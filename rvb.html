Equity vs. Invested Capital Growth
Net home equity (after closing costs) vs. investing the same capital over 10 years
Net home equity = appreciated home value − remaining loan balance (proper amortization, front-loaded interest) − closing costs. Starts below your down payment because closing costs are immediately sunk. Invested capital = your down payment + closing costs compounded at the opportunity cost rate — what that same money would grow to in the market.
